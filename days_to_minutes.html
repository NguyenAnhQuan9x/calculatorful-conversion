First of all, you should know a little about the history of day and minutes

Day is the amount of time needed for the Earth to complete one complete rotation around its axis. The time value is rounded up to 24 hours and is standardized. As a result, a day generally lasts 86,400 seconds. A day, denoted by the sign d, is the unit of time as it is used in the SI system. This unit is utilized extensively in a wide range of situations, from daily living to scientific requirements.

Minutes is a unit of time, used widely around the world beyond just the time measuring purposes (i.e. for defying time standards, time zones, etc.). According to the standards, a minute is equal to 60 seconds, or 1/60th of an hour. The symbol for this time unit is min. A minute is also used as a unit of measuring angle widely used in geometry, astronomy, and other branches of science.
