First of all, you should know a little about the history of feet and meters

A foot (pl.feet) is a standard length unit used in both the present US customary unit system and the Imperial system. 0.3048 meters are equal to one foot. Since the Roman and Greek eras, this length measurement has been used in Europe. A yard is made up of three feet and 12 inches.

A meter is a SI unit that is widely used as the primary measure of length and distance. A meter is one of the basic SI units, along with others like a kilometer or an inch. The distance a light travels in a vacuum over a period of 1/299,792,458 seconds is equal to one meter. One meter, denoted by the SI letter m, is equal to 100 centimeters, or one thousandth (10-3) of a kilometer.
