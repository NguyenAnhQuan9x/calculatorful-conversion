Pounds to Calories Conversion

If you want to lose weight and reduce body fat, you need to use more calories than you consume, creating what's called a calorie deficit. This is frequently achieved by either lowering your caloric intake, raising your caloric expenditure, or doing both. You must expend the same number of calories as are contained in one pound in order to lose that weight.

How Many Calories Are in a Pound?

In 1958, a scientist named Max Wishnofsky concluded that the caloric equivalent of one pound of body weight lost or gained was 3,500 calories. so therefore it would seem that if you reduce your diet by 500 calories a day or exercise and burn off 500 calories a day (or any combo of the two), that you would lose a pound a week.

How to convert pounds to calories?

To convert a pound measurement to a calorie measurement, multiply the weight by the conversion ratio. Since one pound of body fat is equal to 3,500 calories, you can use this simple formula to convert:

calories = pounds × 3,500

For example, here's how to convert 8 pounds to calories using the formula above.
8 lb = (8 × 3,500) = 28,000 kcal
So, to lose 8 pounds, you would need to burn 28,000 additional calories.
